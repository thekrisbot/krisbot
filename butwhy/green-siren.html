Green Siren

That's that me espresso
March 27, 2026

Stars. Soundtracks. Seas. Starbucks?

There is only one word that best describes daring to breathe Starbucks into the same line as the rest, let alone crown it with the same melancholic reverence.

“Sacrilege”.

Yes, the coffee is too sweet. Too expensive. Too… American? 

And yes, there are better beans roasted by better people in better cafés with better politics.

But for some it is about the same smell. Same hiss of the espresso machine. Same unhurried low hum of a room full of people who have silently agreed not to bother each other. 

Jaded. Jet-lagged. Jittery at the thought of walking into a foreign land by yourself.

Rushed. Overwhelmed. Rejected.

a warm, seductive green siren with your name tattooed on her — just waiting for you to come find her? That's a specific feeling of comfort that your stuffed animal sitting in a box in some attic with its cowboy and space ranger friends watches with a feeling between envy and relief.

To paraphrase an advertising savant, for most, happiness is the smell of a new car. It’s freedom from fear. It’s a billboard on the side of the road that screams reassurance that whatever you are doing is okay. You are okay.

But for the few whose happiness is that “sweet, expensive, American coffee, we don’t demand you love it too. We just ask, you let us do it in peace.
