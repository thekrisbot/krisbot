C · But Why Though

Soul-searching

Dan and Delilah.
March 21, 2026

CHARACTERS:

RYAN: late 20s. Thoughtful. Nice. The kind of guy who has opinions about mundane things and needs you to know it. Currently in the best two weeks of his life all year.

DAN: late 20s. Ryan's friend and roommate. Good guy. Horny. Simple. Has been listening to Ryan talk about his latest crush for days now.

DELILAH: late 20s. Smart. Pretty. More than she lets on.

SETTING: Dan and Ryan's apartment. Followed by the restaurant.

(Lights up. Football on. Ryan on the couch, not watching, phone in hand.)

DAN: Dude. The game! Are you still talking to the Tinder chick?

RYAN: Huh-huh. (mechanically)

DAN: That's it. I gotta see who's got you whipped like this. I haven't seen you get this excited looking at a screen since we tracked down the Kim K tape in sophomore year.

(Dan jumps at Ryan on the couch and swipes the phone, zooming on the profile.)

DAN: (reads the profile) De-Li-lah. Oooof. Dudeee. (shocked by her looks)

RYAN: Dan, stop. You know I don't care about that. She’s… interesting.

DAN: Interesting meaning = HOT.

RYAN: Interesting meaning = Interesting, Dan. Don't you ever talk to a woman and go, damn, I could just listen—

DAN: Fa-lalla-laa booooring. (interrupting, covering his ears)

RYAN: This one is different, Dan. I really mean it.

DAN: (not looking up from the TV) Another Ryan Williams classic. "The One". Featuring Maya.. Aarti… And this week... Delilah.

RYAN: (annoyed) Look at the game, I am going for a walk. 

DAN: (at a distance, as Ryan walks out the door) I am just saying.. There’s plenty of fish in the sea! You don’t stop fishing just because you caught a Marlin! (door slams shut.)

(Blackout.)

(One week later. Lights up. Ryan's bedroom. Late. In bed, lamp on, mid-conversation.)

DELILAH: (text message) "I think people who say they don't care what others think are either lying or haven't found anything worth caring about yet. It's like what Cioran said: 'I exist because I have not yet had time to be nothing.'"

RYAN: Okay, that one's profound. Is there anything you are not well-versed in?

DELILAH: 🥷

RYAN: 😂

(Chat continues into the night.)

(Blackout.)

(Few days later. Lights up. Ryan on the couch, phone out, showing Dan something.)

RYAN: Dan, Dan. Look at what Delilah just sent me.

DAN: A thirst trap? (eyes widening)

RYAN: (rolling his eyes) No. She said.. just, just read this.

DAN: (squinting at the phone) "The reason we romanticise the past is because the memory edits out the waiting." (looks up)

DAN: She's using em dashes?

RYAN: What?

DAN: Right here. (pointing at the text)

RYAN: What about it?

DAN: Dude. Everyone and their dog knows em dashes are code for AI.

RYAN: (taking the phone back) I use em dashes.

DAN: Why? (shocked)

RYAN: Good grammar? ChatGPT didn't invent punctuation, Dan. Maybe if you picked up a book before 2020... or ever. (in a playful mocking tone, with a wink)

DAN: (back to the TV) Ugh, you nerds make me wanna barf.

(Blackout.)

(Lights up. Kitchen. Ryan has finished up dessert preparations. Dan walks by and sticks a finger in and tastes.)

DAN: Mhmm, dude. This is so good, I wanna slather some of it on Kiana.

RYAN: First of all, that's disgusting on so many levels. And Kiana? What happened to Emily?

(Dan shrugs.)

RYAN: And thank Delilah. She sent me this whole thing about how the trick is to let the custard come to room temperature before you torch it for that extra... (gesturing chef's kiss)

DAN: Oh that's so hot. I hope you used protection. (deadpan, sarcastically)

(Ryan rolls his eyes.)

RYAN: Dan, I think I might actually be falling for this girl. 

DAN: Then meet her, dummy! Look, girls with a big... (gesturing towards the chest then seeing Ryan's face) brain like that? They don't stay on the market for long. Just ask her out for dinner.

RYAN: Actually? I did. (sheepishly)

DAN: Dude. Seriously? When? What'd she say?? (unable to contain his excitement)

RYAN: We are going to L'Artusi. This Friday.

DAN: (hugging and pressing his shoulder) You son of a gun. You thought it was important to let me know what her favourite Naruto poem was—

RYAN: Neruda! Pablo Neruda!

DAN: Gawwwwd. Whatever. And you didn't think to tell me you were FINALLY going to PORKTOWN!?

RYAN: Porkto—? Ugh, it's like I am living with a dog in heat. It's just dinner. And I didn't tell you because I didn't wanna jinx it, okay?

DAN: Alright. (Dan walks away, mumbling in excitement.)

(Blackout.)

(L'Artusi. Friday. 7:30PM. Delilah arrives.)

(She is stunning. Ryan stands. They land on a hug.)

RYAN: Hi! (nervously)

DELILAH: Hi. (confidently)

RYAN: Okay I have to say. The Neruda thing. I went and read the whole collection after you sent that.

DELILAH: Why thank you. Sorry, I am.. (slightly blushing)

RYAN: What? Is everything okay?

DELILAH: (calculated smile) No. It's just.. I don't think I have heard men open with that. It's usually about my hair or my (gesturing at her face) or my outfi--

RYAN: Oh shoot! I am so sorry! You look beauti--

DELILAH: Ryan! I wasn’t fishing for a compliment. It's nice. It's refreshing.

(Ryan smiles awkwardly, bumbling. Accidentally knocks over the wine glass. Apologises.)

(An hour passes. They share dessert. Ryan mid-conversation.)

RYAN: (taking a bite of his raspberry pie) Oh, this is amazing. Oh btw, the crème brûlée trick? Dan keeps asking me if I can make it again. Thanks again for the tip. 

DELILAH: Oh, good.

RYAN: (dessert still in mouth, holding the fork animatedly) Maybe it’s the wine. Or this pie. But.. the thing you said about vulnerability. I keep coming back to that.

(Delilah looks at her drink. Something moving behind her eyes.)

RYAN: What?

DELILAH: Nothing. I'm just.. I haven't had a date like this in… (thinks) gosh, forever.

RYAN: (nervously) In a… good way?

DELILAH: (smiling) Depends on how you read ‘different’. 

(She looks at him for a moment.)

DELILAH: Ryan.

RYAN: Yeah?

DELILAH: I need to tell you something.

RYAN: Everything all right? (nervously)

DELILAH: (sighs) I don't know. (a single tear rolls down that she quickly wipes away)

RYAN: Okay, you are making me a little nervous

DELILAH: No.. it's just.. I know how to talk to guys. (holding back)

RYAN: That's a good thing, milady. (tipping his imaginary cap)

(She laughs nervously at his attempt to lighten the mood.)

DELILAH: No! I mean GUYS… you know? Guys who confidently slide into your DMs with a shirtless mirror selfie as their opening line. 

Guys who can't remember your cat's name but know the exact color of the flower on your underwear. 

(Breaking down slightly, wiping her face before the tears fully form.) 

Guys who CAN’T WAIT for you to finish telling them how your day went so they can start asking how wet you are for them.

(Ryan reaches across the table. Doesn't say anything. Just leaves his hand there.)

RYAN: Wow. I am.. Sorry. I really am. And you are right. Most of us are exactly what you described. I know guys like that. Good guys. And they just switch off the moment the conversation gets past a certain depth. Like there's a ceiling. It's embarrassing. 

RYAN: As a representative of the gender, I'm going to write a formal apology on behalf of everyone. I'll circulate it. 

DELILAH: I'll sign it. (She laughs. Small but real.)

RYAN: For what it's worth. You are going to find someone who shows up for you. And all of you. The hotdog thing. The 3AM roasting of the Irish. Hey, I don't necessarily mean me. (beat) I'm clearly not ready for someone this interesting. Trust me. I'd embarrass myself. Half the things you said, I had to Google to see what you meant.

Oops. (smiling sheepishly.)

(She listens intently. Eyes struggling to contain what she wouldn't say.)

RYAN: But you can’t tell me that I am the only guy who lost twenty minutes down a rabbit hole because of something you said. The Cioran thing alone. I couldn’t stop thinking about it for two days!

DELILAH: Ryan.

RYAN: Yeah?

DELILAH: I'm sorry.

RYAN: What for? (puzzled)

DELILAH: I don't think this can go anywhere.

RYAN: (caught off guard) What? Why?

DELILAH: I just. I don't think it can.

RYAN: I'm confused. We're having a good time, you just said you haven't had a date like this in forever, and now—

DELILAH: I know. I'm sorry.

RYAN: Did I say something wrong?

DELILAH: No. You said everything right. That's the problem. All those guys I was cribbing about? I know exactly what they want and I know exactly how to give them just enough to keep them interested. (using up the tissues on the counter.) 

But I don't know how to talk to guys who appreciate your weird "is a hotdog just a really good looking sandwich" dumb brainrot and actually mean it.

(She looks at him. He's genuinely confused. She closes her eyes for a second.)

RYAN: But.. Isn’t that a good thing? I mean, I can see how it is new and confusing but isn’t what also makes it.. Exciting? It’s like that Mrs.Maisel show you love. Why wouldn’t you want to—

DELILAH: BECAUSE IT’S NOT ME, RYAN! (fork clattering on the plate, as people leer.)

RYAN: What? (confused)

DELILAH: The poem. The recipe. The dumb quote. Every single detail you just listed. None of it is me. 

RYAN: I don’t understand. What do you mean it's not you? (befuddled.)

DELILAH: Yeah. I just typed your messages into ChatGPT and sent back whatever it gave me. That's it. (Silence. It lands. Slowly. All of it.)

RYAN: What do you mean? 

DELILAH: It means for two weeks I have been leading you on, for my own sadistic pleasure. 

(Beat. He looks at the table.)

RYAN: It can’t be. I mean… Why would? And how would it even..? Like—

DELILAH: It means I sent your chat and asked it to write a poem and picked the one that sounded most.. “Poety”. It means copy pasting whatever you said about your grandma and saying something "profound". 

(Long silence. Reality sinking in)

RYAN: But why? Why go to such great lengths to impress a guy like me when you could walk into  any room and—

DELILAH: And have someone decide who I am before I’ve even said a word? 

(Long silence)

DELILAH: Trust me, Ryan. You have no idea what it’s like to live your entire life scared.. wondering if the people who like you, would even talk to you if you didn't look the way you did.

(chuckling, tears rolling down)

And I don't know if there's anything underneath all this (gesturing to her face) that's worth staying for. 

(Just the ambient noise of the restaurant.)

RYAN: (bargaining) But tonight! When you were talking about the guys in your DMs. When you were crying. This. This is you.

DELILAH: Yeah?

RYAN: So there is something. Underneath all of it there is an actual person who feels actual things and I just... (trails off)

(Long pause. Delilah leaning in hoping he'd finish his thought.)

DELILAH: And honestly? Whatever existential crisis this is? It's for me to work on and I will never forgive myself for dragging you into this mess.

RYAN: But I want to—

DELILAH: Ryan, please. I’ll always be grateful that you walked into my life. The two weeks of texting? I might not have meant what I said. But I certainly felt the weight of every single thing you did. And for me that's... (searching for the right word.) yes, rare. 

But whatever this is? (pointing at each other) This is not how a new romance or a… (pausing) first date.. wow I can’t believe I dumped all of this on someone I am meeting for the first time (smiling at the realization). 

But. I hope you know how much this meant to me. Today. The last two weeks. You. 

(Delilah picks up her bag and plants a kiss on his cheek)

I'm just going to freshen up and then I'll head off. 

(She walks toward the back. Ryan sits alone. Stares at his drink.)

(Calls Dan. Voicemail) 

RYAN: (voicemail to Dan) Hey man, the date was.. (sighing) a total bust. I’ll explain everything in-person. I’ll see you in an hour. (Sets the phone face down.)

RYAN: (to himself): Plenty of fish, huh?

(He looks toward the back of the restaurant. And then the entrance. Distracted.)

(dials)

RYAN: Hey! Tiffany? Do you wanna get dinner tomorrow night?  

(a voice responding on the phone)

Great. I heard L'Artusi is great. I'll see you at 7:30?

(Blackout.)
