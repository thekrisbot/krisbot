Gay Bar

Marco is ready to come out...
March 8, 2026

CHARACTERS:

MARCO — late 20s, floral button-up, slim trousers, a ceramic mug in a little tote bag. Expressive, warm, volcanic. Has the aesthetic of a man who knows every word to "Defying Gravity" and the dating history of someone who has been doing it very, very wrong. This is his first gay bar. He is hopeful. He is already losing hope. It has been forty-five minutes.

DEREK — late 20s, broad-shouldered, black t-shirt, dark jeans, clean white sneakers. Still water. The kind of guy who fills a room without trying. Genuinely warm underneath the cool. Has been to many bars in his life and never once asked himself why this kind of bar always feels the most like coming home.

JESS & KAYLA — bachelorette party. Sashes, light-up crowns, a mission.

CROCS GUY — bowl cut, rubber duck charm, absolute confidence.

SETTING: A gay bar. Neon lights, upbeat pop music. A high-top table near the bar.

(Lights up. Marco is mid-spiral, gesturing wildly with his ceramic mug. Derek drinks his beer. Still. Easy.)

MARCO: Forty-five minutes, Derek. FORTY-FIVE MINUTES. Not one person. Not a glance. Not an accidental brush of the hand reaching for bar nuts—

DEREK: Were you reaching for bar nuts hoping someone would—

MARCO: I was creating OPPORTUNITIES. (gestures across the room) That guy over there - bowl cut, Crocs with little charms on them, I can see a rubber duck and what I believe is a miniature waffle — he has been in three conversations tonight. THREE. How, Derek. How.

DEREK: Maybe he's funny.

MARCO: I'm funny! I'm warm, I'm funny, I drove to three different Targets for this shirt - there was a Paramus involved, Derek, I crossed state lines!! And that man has a RUBBER DUCK on his shoe and he's doing GREAT!

DEREK: You're trying too hard.

MARCO: I'm not trying hard ENOUGH, clearly…

DEREK: No. There's a difference. (leans back, easy) You walk in like you're waiting to be chosen. Like you're already apologizing for being in the room.

MARCO: I'm not apologizing.

DEREK: You asked the bouncer if he'd had a long night.

MARCO: I was being warm!

DEREK: And offered him a sip of your mug.

MARCO: It's chamomile! I have acid reflux when I'm nervous!

DEREK: You brought chamomile tea to a bar. 

MARCO: It's in a NICE MUG. (beat, deflating) I just don't understand. Six years I dated women. Nothing. Not one relationship that made sense. And everyone kept saying… "Marco, you're so sensitive." "Marco, why do you know the full choreography to the 'Single Ladies' video." "Marco, a grown man should not have a Pinterest board called Future Vibes with three hundred and forty pins—"

DEREK: Three hundred and— (in shock)

MARCO: There's a whole Coastal Grandfather section, Derek. The point is, at some point you have to listen to the universe. And the universe looked at me (gestures at entire self) — the floral shirt, the mug, the Pinterest board - and said PLAY FOR THE OTHER TEAM, MARCO!

DEREK: (small smile) So here we are.

MARCO: So here we are. New chapter. Fresh start. (squares shoulders, then immediately slumps) And I have been invisible for forty-five minutes.

DEREK: Because you're performing. The moment you walk through any door, you decide you belong there. You don't look around to check if it's okay. You already know.

MARCO: That's easy for you to.. looking like… (stops himself) Anyway, how do you just DO that?

DEREK: Practice. Then it stops being practice. (leans forward) First thing I do when I walk into a room, I don't look for someone to talk to. I read the room. Who's settled, who's still performing, who actually wants to be approached. Thirty seconds, you know everything you need to know.

MARCO: And then what?

DEREK: Then you don't rush it. You let things develop. The best nights I've ever had, nothing happened fast.

MARCO: Okay. (nodding slowly) Okay that's..yeah. Slow burn. Like "Normal People." Have you seen "Normal People?"

DEREK: No.

MARCO: Derek, it's on Hulu, it's twelve episodes—

DEREK: Can we stay focused?

MARCO: It will RUIN you in the best possible way, there's this one scene on a staircase where—

DEREK: Marco.

MARCO: Right. Sorry. Tips. Keep going.

DEREK: When you talk to someone, don't lead with yourself. Ask about them. What they care about, what they're excited about, what they did today they didn't expect. People want to feel like someone is genuinely interested in who they are. Like, if I were to talk to the Crocs guy, I’d ask about the unironic Nike stickers—

MARCO: Jibbitz, but go on.

DEREK: You'd be surprised how rarely that happens.

MARCO: (fishing for pen in tote bag) I want to write that down, I have a notebook with a William Morris print. (silently gesturing excitement)

DEREK: Put it away.

MARCO: I retain things better when I write—

DEREK: If you pull out a William Morris notebook right now, no one will ever approach you.

MARCO: (putting it away) Fine. Fine. Keep going.

DEREK: The other thing. And this one's important. You know that feeling when someone knows something about you? Something small. Something you mentioned in passing and assumed they'd forgotten. And then they bring it back.

MARCO: (quietly) Yeah.

DEREK: That's everything. That's the whole game. People remember when you remember them.

MARCO: Derek. Where did you learn all this?

DEREK: (shrugs) Paying attention.

(A beat. Marco looks around the room. Sighs heavily.)

MARCO: I just.. I want what everyone wants. Someone to go to the farmer's market with. Split a focaccia. Watch something on a Sunday and have feelings about it. (romantic gesture animated)

DEREK: You'll get there.

MARCO: You don't know that!

MARCO: (quietly, humming a song to himself) Mm-mm-mm... long story short... I survived... mm-mm... I was running—

DEREK: Screaming.

MARCO: Sorry?

DEREK: Screaming. "I was screaming." Not running.

MARCO: (very still) ...What?

DEREK: The lyric. "Long story short, I survived. Long story short, I was screaming." That’s the line.

MARCO: (catching himself, quieter) Derek. I didn't know you were into Taylor Swift.

DEREK: (shrugs) 

MARCO: (staring, something dawning) God. Why couldn't you have been—

DEREK: (cutting him off smoothly) Which actually brings me to my next point. Speak their language. Learn what they like. Taylor Swift. Cats. Potpourri. It can't hurt you. But it could help you.

MARCO: (gestures at entire self) Look at me!! I majored in *that* language. 

DEREK: (small beat) That's true. (scanning Marco quickly)

(A beat. Neither of them looks at the other.)

DEREK: I have a feeling your fortunes are going to change tonight.

(Marco opens his mouth. Closes it. Looks around the room.)

(A BACHELORETTE PARTY tumbles past - sashes, light-up crowns. JESS and KAYLA, tipsy, stop at the table, scanning the room like they've been on a mission.)

JESS: Okay hi. Hi! (giggling) we are so sorry to interrupt. We've been here two hours for our friend's bachelor party and we cannot get a whiff of a straight man in this entire bar—

KAYLA: (leans in, conspiratorial) Until we saw your table.

(They are both looking directly at Marco. Marco looks at Derek.)

MARCO: (pointing at himself) ...Me? (mouths) 

KAYLA: It's a compliment! Here. (slides napkin) My number. Hers is on the back.

JESS: Text either of us. Or both. We don’t mind sharing. (more giggles)

(They float back into the party. Marco holds the napkin. Stares at it. Turns it over.)

MARCO: Derek...Two women just gave me their number.

DEREK: (calm) Yeah!

MARCO: In a gay bar. (looking up slowly) With you sitting next to me. And they thought I was the straight one?? Wait, does that mean I am actually not..? (glances up noticing a man walking towards him). Oh god, here comes the rubber duck.. Quick, look busy!

(CROCS GUY appears at the table. Rubber duck. Miniature waffle. Total serenity. He slides a napkin in front of Derek without breaking stride.)

CROCS GUY: Hey. I like your jeans. They are tight in the right places. (hands off a napkin to… Derek.) (He walks away. Marco and Derek both look down at their napkins. Then up at each other. A long beat.)

MARCO: (very slowly) Derek.

DEREK: (looking at the napkin)

MARCO: That man just gave you his number.

DEREK: (folds it once, slides it into his back pocket)

MARCO: In a gay bar.

DEREK: (stands, straightens jacket)

MARCO: (the full realization arriving) And two beautiful straight women just ignored you and gave ME their numbers. Derek. Derek. (Derek looks at him. A long beat. Something quiet and certain behind his eyes.) Are you….?

(Derek shrugs. Gets up unhurried towards the direction of the Crocs Guy.)

MARCO: (watching him go, then to himself) "Long story short... I was screaming." (Beat.)

MARCO: DEREK!

(Blackout.)
