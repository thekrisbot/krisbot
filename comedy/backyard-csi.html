Backyard CSI

whodunnit?
March 31, 2026

CHARACTERS:

PATRICIA — Early 40s. Paranoid, dramatic, loving. Cannot let anything go.

STEVE — Mid 40s. Has been married long enough to know exactly when to speak and exactly what not to say. Mostly chooses neither.

REESE — 16. Not stupid. Stupid about school. Smart about everything that serves him.

RICK — 7. Has read a book about werewolves. A proper one. He knows what he knows.

CELIA — 11. Neighbour. Came to return a Tupperware. Now regretting it.

JAZ — The cat. Briefly a suspect. Unbothered.

SETTING: The back garden. Monday morning. A dried blood trail runs from the back gate across the patio, past the shed, toward the back wall.

(Patricia is in her dressing gown, crouched over the blood trail near the gate, phone torch on. 

PATRICIA: Steve!! Steve come and look at this. Come and look at this right now.

STEVE: (coming over, looking at the trail) Coming! What do we got here?

PATRICIA: It goes all the way to the wall. Look. Gate to wall. (pointing to the blood trail)

STEVE: (following it with his eyes) Hm. 

PATRICIA: “Hm?” That’s all you have to say?

STEVE: REESE! Happy? (Pat rolls her eyes.)

PATRICIA: REESE. RICK. BACKYARD. NOW.

REESE: (from inside) Mom, it’s 7 in the morning. I have to get ready for school. 

PATRICIA: SHUT YOUR MOUTH AND GET DOWN HERE RIGHT NOW. BRING YOUR BROTHER TOO.

(Rick comes out first, rubbing his eyes, half-asleep. Reese appears in the doorway annoyed. 

He sees the blood trail. Goes completely still.)

REESE: (slowly) ...Is that blood?

PATRICIA: Yes. Hands. Both of you, come here.

REESE: (not moving, taking in the full length of it) That is a lot of blood.

PATRICIA: REESE—

(He comes. She grabs his wrists, turns them over, checks his arms. He lets her, still looking at the trail over her shoulder. Steve watches.)

STEVE: (to Reese, mild) Rough night?

PATRICIA: Steve! 

STEVE: Hey, you wanted my help. (shrugs).

(Patricia moves straight to Rick, checking him the same way.)

PATRICIA: Did you boys get in trouble last night?

RICK & REESE No (in unison.) (Rick puts his hand up.)

PATRICIA: Huh. If it isn’t the kids then—

RICK: Mom. I know what it is. (arm up in excitement, eyes widening)

PATRICIA: You do? Come here, sweetie. (crouching right down to his level, planting a kiss)

RICK: (completely certain) It’s a werewolf!

(Beat. Patricia looks at him. He looks back. Sincere.)

PATRICIA: (straightening up slowly, gently) Sweetheart. We’ve talked about this. Werewolves aren't real… Reese, have you been telling your brother scary stories again? (sternly looking at Reese)

REESE: WHAT? NO! I didn’t tell him nothing. It’s that stupid big book he got from the school lib—

PATRICIA: Sweetie, why don't you go have your cereal? I’ll come in a few minutes (crouching down, holding Rick’s shoulders)

RICK: But, mom. If you look at the blood pattern—

PATRICIA: The gate was unlatched this morning. (already moving, already talking to Steve.) 

(Rick drifts next to Reese while Patricia and Steve talk.)

RICK: (barely audible) Don't you have a history test today?

REESE: (shushing) Don't.

RICK: Mom—

REESE: (grabbing Rick’s arms and quietly warning) Rick. You know the big spider in our room? I know where exactly it is now. I can introduce him to your bed tonight.

(Rick looks at him scared and backs down. Reese looks at the trail, face completely neutral.)

PATRICIA: (to Steve) Could it be Jaz? Where is Jaz? Has anyone seen her since last night?

JAZ. JAZ COME HERE—

(Silence. Then Jaz walks out from behind the shed and sits down. Begins cleaning her face.)

PATRICIA: (checking her over) She's fine. She's completely- (checking her paws)- yeah. Fine.

STEVE: Shocking.

PATRICIA: (standing, looking at the trail) STEVE! Cut out your passive aggressive mocking and help me out here. This is not funny.

STEVE: What do you want from me, Pat? Something came in last night and got hurt. Simple. This is not an episode of Law & Order. Can we just go back in? I have to get ready for work. The kids have school… we’ve already been here for 20 minutes.

(silence. Everyone is looking at each other. Patricia looks back at Reese with suspicion)

REESE: Oh you're serious right now.

STEVE: We didn't say anything.

REESE: Mom did the look—

PATRICIA: Reese—

REESE: No, I'm sorry, this is.. that is genuinely offensive. You think I did that?

STEVE: Nobody said—

REESE: That's a lot of blood, Dad. Do you know how much planning that would take? The logistics alone—

PATRICIA: What are you talking about?

REESE: I'm making a point. If I did something like this.. (silence) which I didn't… it would be beautiful. And convincing. Not this sloppy work… I am not dumb okay? This is the work of an amat—

(A beat.)

RICK: (quietly, to no one, with a small gesture toward Reese) Mm.

REESE: (to Rick) What.

RICK: Nothing. You scored a D in your last biology test. (looks back at his book)

REESE: (to Patricia) It's not me. Okay? Look at those drops. They are in a perfect straight line. Whatever it was, it knew exactly where it was going. That's not a prank. 

PATRICIA: (to Steve) He does have a point.

PATRICIA: (already moving, already spiralling) Okay. Not Jaz. Not Reese. Something came through that gate. Something big enough to leave that trail. (the thought arriving) Steve.

(Steve exhausted and unable to leave)

PATRICIA: What if someone was hurt. What if someone came through that gate on purpose and—

REESE: (very carefully, very calmly) A hobo!

PATRICIA: I'm not saying hobo—

REESE: No you're absolutely right. That’s a great observation mom. We have to consider it. A hobo. Bleeding. In our garden. In the night. Mum. I cannot in good conscience—

PATRICIA: Reese, I don’t know where you are going with this…

REESE: Give yourself some credit. (leaning in, low and serious) What do we actually know about what's out there? At night. In this neighbourhood.

PATRICIA: (leaning in slightly despite herself) ...What do you mean?

REESE: Dad and I were watching this episode on Dark Yard. And there’s a hobo who killed people and would bury them in random backyards…

(Patricia looks at Steve.)

STEVE: Stalker Gary. He moved to Dorset—

PATRICIA: STEVE! Is this what you watch with the kids? 

STEVE: What? I am sorry. It had a 7.5 on iMDB!

PATRICIA: (scoffs) You are unbelievable. And we wonder why Reese thinks burglaries are cool and Rick believes in leprechauns and werewolves.

(Reese sidles very quietly over to Rick.)

REESE: (barely a whisper) Hey. You know something else about Gary?

RICK: (whispering) What?

REESE: He specifically kids who eat green M&Ms.

(Rick goes very still.)

RICK: (small voice) ...I eat green M&Ms.

REESE: (nodding slowly) I know.

RICK: (to Patricia, genuine panic) Mom. Mom! Is that true? I don’t want to eat M&Ms anymore (tearing up)

PATRICIA: (turning) What? REESE. Stop messing with your brother. 

PATRICIA: (crouching to Rick) It is not true, baby. You are completely safe. Reese is being an idiot.

(Rick goes back to his book. Reese smirks having wasted a few more minutes)

PATRICIA: (back on the trail, back on the spiral) Okay. So. Not Jaz. Not the kids. Something came through. Something big. And it knew how to work the gate.

STEVE: (considering) Marty next door had something get into his garden last summer.

PATRICIA: (very carefully then gasping) ...Marty!

STEVE: What?

PATRICIA: Where does he go every Sunday morning?

STEVE: Golf. Pat—

PATRICIA: Every Sunday? Have you ever actually seen him play golf?

STEVE: I've seen him carry the clubs—

PATRICIA: That is not the same thing. (snapping fingers)

STEVE: Patricia. I am pretty sure our sixty-three year old next door neighbour of fourteen years didn’t pick a fight in our backyard at 2 in the morning. Even If he wanted to, I promise you he would not choose the garden of the woman who plays forensics over a dead pigeon.

(Beat.)

PATRICIA: Excuse me? What are you implying?

STEVE: Nothing. You know what? I take it back. Let’s add Marty to the suspects list.

PATRICIA: Don’t act smart, Steve. If you have something to say, spit it out. (arms crossed).

STEVE: All I am saying is you have a tendency to… (searching for the right word) blow things out of proportion—

PATRICIA: There it is! I am sorry I am the only one in the family who is bothered about the pool of blood in my backyard right below where my two sons sleep, Steve. What a monster! (in tears)  

STEVE: Pat. You know I didn’t mean that way. I am just saying, sometimes we need to pick our battles. And maybe this isn’t the best use of our time on a Monday morning. 

(hugging Pat and signalling the kids to get inside.)

PATRICIA: You are right. I am sorry. Reese, get your bag. If we skip the back roads and there are no red lights we can still make your history test.

(Reese stands in the garden.)

REESE: (very quietly) Oh come on.

STEVE: (not unkindly) Tough luck. You were almost there, buddy. (looks at his watch.)

(Reese trudges inside. Steve stands alone in the garden. Picks up and looks at the full moon illustration from Rick's book. Sets the book down. Goes inside.)

(Blackout.)

END TAG

(The same garden. Last night. 2am. Full moon.)

(The gate swings open - carefully, the latch worked from outside. A WEREWOLF comes through low and fast, bleeding from a gash in its side. A SECOND WEREWOLF drops from the top of the wall behind it.)

(They collide near the shed. The first swipes - three deep gouges in the felt roof. They grapple. Blood across the patio stones. Dark hair catches on the rough brick as they clear the wall together. The sounds disappear down the street.)

(Blackout.)
