24K Magic - A Bruno Mars misadventure

CHARACTERS:

TYLER — mid 20s, aspiring comedian. Genuinely funny on paper. Genuinely clueless about music. 

JAKE — mid 20s, the music guy. Knows everything about music. Always inspiring.

SETTING: A bar. Two beers. Tyler's comedy notebook open on the table. Jake has just finished reading.

(Lights up. Jake sets the notebook down carefully.)

JAKE: Tyler. This is really good. Like network tv good.

TYLER: Yeah?

JAKE: You know who this reminds me of? Bruno.

TYLER: Who?

JAKE: Mars.

TYLER: Sure, I'll take one. I love the little gold wrappers. My mom used to put them in my stocking at Christmas— (flagging the bartender) 

JAKE: That's—no. I'm not talking about the candy. Bruno Mars. The musician. 

TYLER: (snapping fingers) Oh! The guy from Maroon 5! 

JAKE: What? No. He does “Uptown Funk”—

TYLER: (lighting up) Billy Joel!

JAKE: That's—

TYLER: So Bruno Mars is Billy's nickname?

JAKE: No. Why would Billy Joel be called Bruno Mars?

TYLER: I don't know, why would anyone be called Slim Shady?

JAKE: Eh, fair point. But no. Billy Joel is not Bruno Mars. 

And you're thinking of “Uptown Girl”. (beat) Forget that one. Ooh.. (excited) he wrote “Just The Way You Are”. Surely you've heard that one?

TYLER: (face opening up) Ohhh I LOVE that song. The fedora guy!

JAKE: (slapping the table, leaning forward) YES. The fedora guy. Finally—

TYLER: (standing slightly, one hand out, crooning with full sincerity) "Someday, when I'm awfully low... when the world is cold... I will feel a glow just thinking of you... and the way you are tonight."

(He sits back down. Moved by his own performance.)

JAKE: (hand on the temple) That's... “The Way You Look Tonight”. Frank Sinatra.

TYLER: (genuinely surprised) Is it?

JAKE: It is a completely different song. Different title. Different melody. Different decade. 

TYLER: They both have "the way" in them.

JAKE: (through his teeth) Bruno Mars is his own person. Not a nickname. Not a candy bar. (breath) Moving on. He started as a songwriter. Wrote for Cee Lo—

TYLER: (gently correcting) Uh, Jakey. It's Celine. I gotta say, that's a pretty basic mistake for a music guy like you.

JAKE: What? No. Not Celine. Cee Lo.

TYLER: (blank stare)

JAKE: Green—

TYLER: (eyes widening) We can get weed here?

JAKE: NOT THAT GREEN. It's a NAME. Cee Lo Green. He sings Forget You—

TYLER: Well he sounds like Celine (shrugs)

JAKE: No. Cee Lo Green and Celine Dion are COMPLETELY DIFFERENT HUMANS. DIFFERENT GENDERS. One does Forget You. The other does the Titanic. Never met. Never will. 

TYLER: “Every night in my dreams… I see you..” (off-key)

JAKE: Yes.

TYLER: (beat) So Bruno Mars wrote “My Heart Will Go On”?

JAKE: Ye—what? NO. 

TYLER: (beat) Then why would you bring it up?

JAKE: (losing himself) I didn’t!!! (breathes) Let me just get it out. Bruno Mars wrote for other people for years. Nobody saw him. Then the world figured out he was a star. That is what I'm saying about your writing.

TYLER: (genuinely moved) Jake. Man.

JAKE: I mean it. And here's the thing — Bruno didn't just stay a writer. He got inspired by James Brown and that is why he stepped out. Watched that man perform and thought — I need to be up there. That's the energy he brought to the stage—

TYLER: (lighting up) The furniture guy!

JAKE: What?

TYLER: (already singing, fully committed) "Don't pay a cent in the month of September, James Brown Furniture World—"

JAKE: That is a JINGLE—

TYLER: (still going, louder) "No money down, no interest till December—"

JAKE: TYLER—

TYLER: (finishing, settling back with satisfaction) Very catchy. My parents got their sectional there. Held up beautifully.

JAKE: James Brown is the Godfather of Soul. One of the most important musicians who ever lived—

TYLER: AND he does furniture?

JAKE: DIFFERENT JAMES BROWN—

TYLER: Ah. Must be rough at parties. "Hi I'm James Brown." "THE James Brown?" "No, the sofa one."

JAKE: (despite himself) That's actually funny. See, this is what I mean. You are good at this stuff. Anyway. Let me show you a picture. One picture. (Jake pulls out his phone.)

You'll recognize him immediately because he's one of the most famous people alive.

(Waits. Taps. Waits.)

TYLER: Is it loading?

JAKE: It's loading.

(More waiting.)

TYLER: The WiFi here is terrible.

(Jake turns the phone off and on. Still loading. He looks up. Spots a guy crossing the bar with what looks like a guitar case. He sits up.)

JAKE: Sir! Hey — sir!

(Guitar Guy stops.)

JAKE: You look like a cultured person. My friend doesn't know who Bruno Mars is. Can you help me out?

GUITAR GUY: (looks at Tyler) Oh damn. My dad was a huge fan.

JAKE: (warmly) Oh — I'm so sorry.

GUITAR GUY: What for?

JAKE: Your dad’s passing...

GUITAR GUY: What? My dad is not dead. He lives in Scottsdale.

JAKE: But you said he was a huge fan. He doesn't listen to Bruno anymore?

GUITAR GUY: Oh I see the confusion. My dad is doing fine.. It’s Bruno who is dead. 

JAKE: WHAT. HE IS ABSOLUTELY NOT—

GUITAR GUY: Yeah. Wrestled in the sixties. Huge guy. Sold out Madison Square Garden. Fans called him Bruno Mars. Fondly. (solemnly) Rest in peace.

JAKE: (standing, both hands on the table) That. Is. Bruno. SAMMARTINO. Bruno Mars is a MUSICIAN. He is ALIVE. He headlined the Super Bowl. He has won SIXTEEN GRAMMYS. And I mean no disrespect to the man but he is NOT — he has NEVER been — he will NEVER be Bruno MARS—

(Beat. Guitar Guy and Tyler exchange a look.)

GUITAR GUY: (quietly, to Tyler) Is he okay?

TYLER: He's having a day.

JAKE: (turning to Guitar Guy, gesturing at the case) You have a GUITAR. How do you not know Bruno Mars?

GUITAR GUY: (looks down at the case) Oh this isn't a guitar. (unzipping the case) It's my pickleball racket. Found the case in my dad's studio, it fit perfectly.

(Silence.)

JAKE: (sits down slowly)

GUITAR GUY: Anyway man, I gotta go. Good luck. (rolling eyes at Tyler) (walks away)

(Jake stares at his phone. His face changes.)

JAKE: (quietly) It loaded. (holds up the phone) Tyler. Tyler. This is Bruno Mars. Look. Look. 

(Tyler stares at the screen. Guitar Guy drifts back, curious. Both of them look. Long beat.)

TYLER: Ah... this guy.

(Jake puts his phone down. Done. Finished. Accepts it.)

JAKE: You still don't know who he is, do you?

TYLER: Nope.

JAKE: You know what. Forget Bruno Mars. The point — the only point — is that your writing is good. That's all I wanted to say.

TYLER: (beat, something clicking, sitting forward) Wait.

JAKE: What.

TYLER: What you're saying is... I could just start as a writer. Behind the scenes. And then one day step out and actually become a performer. Like that's a real path.

JAKE: YES! That is exactly what I have been saying for thirty minutes.

TYLER: That's actually really smart. You know who did that?

JAKE: Bruno—

TYLER: Michael Che! 

(both at the same time)

TYLER: Started as a writer on Saturday Night Live. Then became an on-screen presence. That's the move right there.

JAKE: (standing, emotional, finally vindicated) YES. Michael Che. EXACTLY. Perfect example — he started as a writer and now you cannot get through one Christopher Nolan film without that guy. That could be you one day.

(Beat. Tyler stares at him.)

TYLER: (slowly, setting his beer down) That's Michael Caine.

JAKE: What—

TYLER: Michael. Caine. Sir Michael Caine. Commander of the Order of the British Empire. Alfred in Batman. Dr.Brand in Interstellar. The man has been in films since before your parents were born, Jake!

JAKE: Sorry it was a slip up—

TYLER: Michael Che is from New York. Michael Caine is London. One of them has two Academy Awards. The other one writes jokes about Scarlett Johansson’s anatomy!

JAKE: (speechless, mouth open)

TYLER: One of them is a knight. A KNIGHT, Jake. The Queen of England knighted him. With a sword. On his shoulder. Do you think the King of England is going around knighting jokesters on SNL? “Sir Michael Che” Does that sound right to you? 

JAKE: No—

TYLER: You know what, you have never bothered to learn anything that is important to me (beat) I need a smoke.

(He storms off. Jake watches him go, mouth hanging open.)

(Jake sits alone. Stares at the table. Then at his phone. Bruno Mars still on the screen.)

JAKE: ARE YOU KIDDING ME!?

(Blackout.)
