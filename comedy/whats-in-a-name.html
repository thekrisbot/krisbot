What's in a name?

That went well. Right?
April 1, 2026

CHARACTERS:

BELLA — Late 20s. Licensed therapist. Tina Fey if Tina Fey had dated a vampire at seventeen and never fully recovered. Sharp, fast, and sassy.

EDWARD — Early 30s. Normal man. First therapy session. Came in good faith. Should have Googled her first.

JACOB — Four seconds. No lines needed.

SETTING: A therapist's office. Tasteful, calm, a succulent on the table. Bella sits across from Edward, notepad on knee, reading glasses on, professional smile fully operational.

(Lights up. Edward is settling onto the couch with first-timer energy — slightly stiff, not sure where to put his hands. Bella is poised, pen ready, smile ready.)

BELLA: Welcome. Safe space, confidential, nothing leaves this room. (pen poised) Let's start with the basics. What's your name?

EDWARD: Edward.

(Bella's pen stops. Her smile stays exactly in place but her eyes do something involuntary. She stares at him. He stares back. Fifteen seconds pass.)

EDWARD: ...Hello?

BELLA: (snapping back, too fast, too bright) Great! Strong name. Classic. Timeless.  (writes something, underlines it, flips the notepad face down) So Edward. What brings you in today.

EDWARD: Uh.. okay. Well I've been struggling a lot lately. The main thing is I just cannot sleep. I lie there every night staring outside the window because—

BELLA: (finger guns) There's a guy from your high school sitting in the corner of your bedroom watching you sleep.

EDWARD: What? No… (confused)

BELLA: (snapping back, clicking pen) Of course it's not. That would be insane. (gesturing with hands.) That is not a thing that happens to normal people. (beat) So ED-Ward..why can't you sleep?

EDWARD: I've just got a lot on my mind. Work mainly. I'm terrified I'm going to lose my job and I can't switch my brain..

BELLA: How long have you felt this way at work?

EDWARD: Three years?

BELLA: And before that?

EDWARD: I was at uni.

BELLA: And your colleagues? How old are they roughly?

EDWARD: I don't know, mixed ages, mid twenties to fifties—

BELLA: (nodding, writing) Right, right. And none of them have been, say in the same job for a long time?

EDWARD: I guess.. I know my manager’s been around for like 18 years. I know a guy in finance who’s been at this from before I was born (chuckling) 

BELLA: Right, right. So like from the early 1900s.

EDWARD: What?...No? I am sorry, this is my first time in therapy, are these sessions always this—

BELLA: Funny? Haha, I try to keep it like Eddy. Watch out. Argh (jumping over from her chair mimicking biting before snapping back.) (clicks tongue, pointing at him.) 

EDWARD: Okay. (not knowing how to react, laughing nervously.) So the restructure was announced six months ago and ever since I just have this constant feeling that I'm going to be—

BELLA: (slowly putting her pen down) Replaced.

EDWARD: Exactly. 

EDWARD: Like I could do everything right and someone could just walk in and takeaway everything I’ve worked for—

BELLA: Out of nowhere.

EDWARD: Yes! (relieved at the validation.)

BELLA: So paint me a picture. This someone.. Are they really old? But doesn't look like their age? 

EDWARD: I mean I haven’t exactly pictured—

BELLA: Absolutely jacked. Probably with enough bumble & bumble for a small village. (laughing maniacally, slapping the table, shocking Edward)

EDWARD: (staring at her) Are you okay?

BELLA: (snapping back, picking up pen) Oh of course. I just had another client in a similar situation. Sorry, go on. (writing) Have you spoken to your manager about how you're feeling?

EDWARD: That's the thing. I can never tell what he's thinking. He just looks at me and I have no idea what's going on in his head and it makes me feel like—

BELLA: You wish you could just read his mind.

EDWARD: Exactly!

BELLA: Just be in there. Know exactly what he's thinking every second of every day and how much of it is about you.

EDWARD: Well I don't know about every—

BELLA: (leaning forward, completely sincere) Trust me, Edward. Be careful what you wish for. (long beat) It is a lot.

EDWARD: Has that...happened to you?

BELLA: (snapping back) What? No? (laughing dismissingly.)  This is your session. So. Back to the insomnia. Walk me through a typical night.

EDWARD: I get into bed around eleven, I'm exhausted, and then the moment my head hits the pillow my brain just starts going. What if I get laid off? What if I can't pay rent. What if I have to move back in with my parents?

BELLA: Your parents. Tell me about them.

EDWARD: Oh, they are the best. They're pretty normal. Supportive. My dad's a doctor actually—

BELLA: (pen completely still) ...Is he now?

EDWARD: Yeah, he's been practicing for like twenty years… (side eyeing)

BELLA: Interesting. And where exactly did you grow up, Edward?

EDWARD: Oh, mostly in the West…

BELLA: Like Seattle?? (in a tense tone barely containing the excitement.)

EDWARD: Oh no, my dad hates wet weather. We were in Cali mostly.

BELLA: (exhaling) Oof, that was close. Great. Normal. 

BELLA: (closing her eyes briefly) Good for him. Great. Normal family. Golf. Wonderful. (composes herself) So you're not sleeping. You're scared of losing the job. What does this do to your day to day? How are you functioning?

EDWARD: Honestly I'm running on empty. I feel like I'm performing a version of myself that has it all together and inside I'm just. Falling apart. 

BELLA: Speaking of falling… have you thought about diving off a cliff?

EDWARD: (taken aback) What? No! You mean like self-harm?

BELLA: (very carefully) Oh no. God no. I meant it as a hobby. I hear it’s very popular on the west coast. (raising eyebrows, relieved that she clarified.)

EDWARD: Oh. Wow, I had no clue.

BELLA: (long pause) Hey, you could always try journaling if you are not adventurous! (clicking pen) 

EDWARD: Right. (long pause)

(Edward notices Bella eyeing him with an intensity he’s never seen before.)

EDWARD: ...Why are you looking at me like that.

BELLA: Like what. I'm not looking at you like anything. I'm your therapist. I look at everyone like this. (beat) 

EDWARD: Okay…? (unconvinced)

BELLA: Great. Fantastic. Just checking. (writes something, very deliberately) So. We've covered a lot of ground today. The insomnia, the job anxiety, your doctor parents… who (checks notes) did not live in Forks. I want to leave you with something practical. One thing to try this week.

EDWARD: Okay. Yes. Please. 

BELLA: When you can't sleep. I want you to just. Close your eyes. And ask yourself. Is the man watching me from the corner of my bedroom someone who genuinely has my best interests at heart. Before you do something you regret.

(A long beat.)

EDWARD: There is no man!? (confused and objectively worse-off after the chat.)

BELLA: (standing, smoothing her skirt) Not yet. (snapping back) I mean. Keep the curtains closed. Home security. General advice. (already walking to the door). So same time next week?

EDWARD: Yeah, no. Sure. Let me do that (mumbling “right”)

BELLA: Alright, take care then! Send in my next patient on your way out. 

(Edward stands. Looks at her. Picks up his jacket. Pauses at the door.)

EDWARD: Hey, I am probably overstepping here but… are you seeing someone?

(Bella looks up. For just a moment something real crosses her face. Then it's gone.)

BELLA: Okay, Edward. Listen, you sound like a great guy! And that name alone.. (gesturing chef’s kiss) but—

EDWARD: (taken aback in embarrassment.) What? No. I meant, like seeing a therapist. Anyway, I’ll see you (already walking out the door, not waiting for an answer) Never, you freak! (quietly, to himself.)

(A knock at the door from the next patient.)

BELLA: (full professional composure, pen poised, smile ready) Come in.

(Door opens. Man sits down on the couch. Looks at her pleasantly.)

BELLA: Welcome. Safe space, confidential, nothing leaves this room. (pen poised) What's your name?

JACOB: Jacob.

(Bella's pen stops.)

(Blackout.)
