Tinder match

Dan hearts Delilah.
March 21, 2026

CHARACTERS:

RYAN: late 20s. Thoughtful. Nice. The kind of guy who has opinions about mundane things and needs you to know it. Currently in the best two weeks of his life all year.

DAN: late 20s. Ryan's friend and roommate. Good guy. Horny. Simple. Has been listening to Ryan talk about his latest crush for days now.

DELILAH: late 20s. Smart. Pretty. The perfect woman for Dan, by his own evaluation.

SETTING: Dan and Ryan's apartment. Followed by the restaurant.

(Lights up. Football on. Ryan on the couch, not watching, phone in hand.)

DAN: Dude. The game! Are you still talking to the Tinder chick?

RYAN: Huh-huh. (mechanically)

DAN: That's it. I gotta see who's got you whipped like this. I haven't seen you get this excited looking at a screen since we tracked down the Kim K tape in sophomore year.

(Dan jumps at Ryan on the couch and swipes the phone, zooming on the profile)

DAN: (reads the profile) De-Li-lah. Oooof. Dudeee. (shocked by her looks) No wonder you haven't looked up once all evening. The perfect body for that name too. (making crude gestures biting his lips)

RYAN: Dan, stop. You know I don't care about that. 

DAN: Gaaaaaaay. So what are you waiting for? It's been two days. Hit it already.

RYAN: Dude. I am not kidding. She's... (shy, blushing) interesting.

DAN: Interesting meaning = HOT.

RYAN: Interesting meaning = Interesting, Dan. Don't you ever talk to a woman and go, damn, I could just listen—

DAN: Fa-lalla-laa booooring. (interrupting, covering his ears)

(Blackout.)

(One week later. Lights up. Ryan's bedroom. Late. In bed, lamp on, mid-conversation.)

DELILAH: (text message) "I think people who say they don't care what others think are either lying or haven't found anything worth caring about yet. It's like what Cioran said: 'I exist because I have not yet had time to be nothing.'"

RYAN: Okay, that one's profound. Is there anything you are not well-versed in?

DELILAH: 🥷

RYAN: 😂

(Chat continues into the night.)

(Blackout.)

(Few days later. Lights up. Ryan on the couch, phone out, showing Dan something.)

RYAN: Dan, Dan. Look at what Delilah just sent me.

DAN: A thirst trap? (eyes widening)

RYAN: (rolling his eyes) No. She said.. just, just read this.

DAN: (squinting at the phone) "The reason we romanticise the past is because the— memory edits out the waiting." (looks up) 

DAN: She's using em dashes?

RYAN: What?

DAN: Right here. (pointing at the text)

RYAN: What about it?

DAN: Dude. Everyone and their dog knows em dashes are code for AI.

RYAN: (taking the phone back) I use em dashes. 

DAN: Why? (shocked)

RYAN: Good grammar? ChatGPT didn't invent punctuation, Dan. Maybe if you picked up a book before 2020... or ever. (in a playful mocking tone, with a wink)

DAN: (back to the TV) Ugh, you nerds make me wanna barf. 

(Blackout.)

(Lights up. Kitchen. Ryan has finished up dessert preparations. Dan walks by and sticks a finger in and tastes.)

DAN: Mhmm, dude. This is so good, I wanna slather some of it on Kiana.

RYAN: First of all, that's disgusting on so many levels. And Kiana? What happened to Emily?

(Dan shrugs.)

RYAN: And thank Delilah. She sent me this whole thing about how the trick is to let the custard come to room temperature before you torch it for that extra... (gesturing chef's kiss)

DAN: Oh that's so hot. I hope you used protection. (deadpan, sarcastically)

(Ryan rolls his eyes.)

RYAN: Dan, I think I might actually like this girl.

DAN: Then meet her, dummy! Look, girls with a big... (gesturing towards the chest then seeing Ryan's face) brain like that? They don't stay on the market for long. Just ask her out for dinner.

RYAN: Actually? I did. (sheepishly)

DAN: Dude. Seriously? When? What'd she say?? (unable to contain his excitement)

RYAN: We are going to L'Artusi. This Friday.

DAN: (hugging and pressing his shoulder) You son of a gun. You thought it was important to let me know what her favourite Naruto poem was—

RYAN: Neruda! Pablo Neruda!

DAN: Gawwwwd. Whatever. And you didn't think to tell me you were FINALLY going to PORKTOWN!?

RYAN: Porkto—? Ugh, it's like I am living with a dog in heat. It's just dinner. And I didn't tell you because I didn't wanna jinx it, okay?

DAN: Alright.(Dan walks away, mumbling in excitement.)

(Blackout.)

(L'Artusi. Friday. 7:30PM. Delilah arrives, catching the attention of every guy around) 

(Ryan stands. They land on a hug.)

RYAN: Hi.

DELILAH: Hi! Oh my god this place is so cute. Have you been before?

RYAN: Once, yeah. Ever since you sent that piece about the origin of Italian—

DELILAH: I love the lighting here. Like, it's doing a lot of heavy lifting and I respect it.

RYAN: (laughing) That's a very specific compliment for a restaurant.

DELILAH: This is a hot take, but when it comes to restaurants, the food can be average if the lighting makes me look good. (chortling)

RYAN: Haha, burn on influencers. (catching her sarcasm)

(Menus. Easy, warm. She's fun.)

RYAN: So! The Neruda poem. I went back and read the whole collection that night! 

DELILAH: Oh yeah? (already scanning the menu) Oh wait, they have burrata. Do you like burrata? We should get the burrata!!

RYAN: Umm sure. The later poems especially, there's something less performed abo—

DELILAH: Oh Ryan, sweetie. Can we get back to literature when I am a few drinks down? I just can't. It's been one of those days.

RYAN: Haha, I am sorry. Of course. But everything all right? (awkward)

DELILAH: Oh god. Where do I begin? Work was insane. Then my roommate adopted a cat without telling anyone, I spilled an entire coffee on myself on the subway...

RYAN: (laughing nervously) That's quite a day. I bet the cat is the most alarming part.

DELILAH: Right? Like just consult me. That's all I ask. (waving in the air)

RYAN: So what kind of cat did she get?

DELILAH: Like an orange one. Very dumb. Already knocked over my favourite candle. 

(Drinks arrive. The conversation meanders but not quite the high they set on texts)

RYAN: Oh. The Cioran quote you sent. I genuinely hadn't come across that one before. Where do you even find these? It's very rare to find people in our generation who can quote philosophy off the cuff like that.

DELILAH: (slight pause, laughs) Oh. Someone sent it to me, I think.

RYAN: Well, your circle is definitely more sophisticated than mine, that I can tell you.

(A visual of Dan inappropriately gesturing flashes briefly.)

DELILAH: (pivoting) Oh sorry, can I steal a bit of that? (gesturing at his bread)

RYAN: Yeah, of course. So do you read much beyond—

DELILAH: (mouth full, eyes closed) Oh, that's so good. Why is bread at restaurants always better than bread at home? Like what are they doing to it?

RYAN: I think it's the—

DELILAH: It's the butter isn't it. It's always the butter. They do something to the butter.

(Ryan nods. Takes a sip of wine as dessert arrives)

RYAN: So do you remember my roommate Dan? You should have seen his face when I showed him what you sent on the memory thing.

DELILAH: (half a beat) Which one was that?

RYAN: Umm remember when we were talking about my grandmother's house and you said how we romanticise the past. Hang on.(pulling up their chat) This one: "The reason we romanticise the past is because memory edits out the waiting." 

DELILAH: Yeah, that's such a good one. (pointing at his dessert) Wait is that the panna cotta? How is it?

RYAN: It's good. (clocking the vibe)

(Beat. Ryan puts his spoon down. Looks at her.)

RYAN: Delilah. This could just be me, but I'm getting a slight... I don't know. A vibe. Like, 'the text you' feels very different to the person in front of me. 

It's probably just the day you had but—

(She looks at him. She puts her spoon down too.)

DELILAH: (sighs) Oh wow, you figured huh. It was my roommate's idea okay? She was like, "dude nobody (tongue out, gesturing typing tediously) on dating apps anymore. 

You just screenshot a profile and give it to ChatGPT and it comes up with like really cool, smart things to say."

RYAN: Wait. WHAT?!

DELILAH: Relax dude, it's not a big deal. I still like edit and stuff before sending.

(Silence.)

RYAN: So you made up the answers to everything I've been asking for two weeks!?

DELILAH: I mean, I still have to tell it things. "Be smart." "Use sad tone." 

RYAN: ...

(Ryan sits back.)

RYAN: I cannot believe this. Dan called it. First week. Saw an em dash on one of your texts and said it was AI and I gave him a whole spiel defending you.

DELILAH: (not unkindly) Ouch. My bad. (shrugging it off casually)

(Beat.)

RYAN: Why would you do that?

DELILAH: (easy shrug) You know how guys areee. Oh you should see some of the stuff they send me on DMs. But this? You are no longer investing on the wrong guy. Match, copy paste, show up, have a drink, see what happens. It's kind of a whole thing I do.

RYAN: And it works?!

DELILAH: You tell me. You are the third guy to bring me here this month.

(Silence. That lands differently than everything else she's said tonight.)

RYAN: (And what do they say after you tell them this? animated) 

DELILAH: Yeah, right. Like they had a clue. They were too busy looking at this. tattoo. (pointing at the unmissable "dream" tattoo on her left chest)

(Ryan, mustering all restraint, ignores it and continues.)

RYAN: Okay, surely all of that couldn't have been made up right? Maybe the archaic quote or poetry. Let's see. Oh. Books! You said your favourite was Don Quixote—

DELILAH: Oh, that's how they say it? I thought it was Don Quick-So-te. (laughing) 

RYAN: Wow. So, what is your day like actually? 

DELILAH: (easy smile) Honestly? Pretty boring. Reality TV. Long baths. Expensive candles. Dates like this. Well (chucking) Not like this. Sorry. (clenching teeth)

RYAN: That can't be.. I mean, the things that we talked. You are so much more than—

DELILAH: Ryan, you are grasping at straws. I am sorry, I lead you on. I really am... but this... (pointing at each other) Can you honestly point at one thing you've seen today that you genuinely find attractive? (silence.)

You like your books. Poetry. Those little action figures you kept sending pictures of. I like those things if I want to pass out and I am all out of booze.

Haven't you ever had a bad date? 

(Ryan nods his head, suggesting no)

Oh, you sweet summer child. (pinching his cheek, as he freezes) 

You have everything mapped out, don't you?

RYAN: But. Why do all this? You clearly don't need to. (gesturing at her face) 

You can probably get any guy you want

DELILAH: (beat, then lightly) Who knows? Maybe it's fun. Maybe I get bored. Maybe some guy broke my heart into a million pieces when I was seventeen and I've been running a low-grade revenge operation on the entire gender ever since.

(She picks up her wine. Sips. Sets it down. Continues talking)

DELILAH: Or maybe because my dad was never there for me and I've been trying to fill the gap in every wrong direction possible.(Sips wine again.)

DELILAH: (Chuckling) It's probably also why most of my first dates end with me sleeping with someone I'd never speak to sober. Waking up and vowing it's the last time I was gonna do this. 

(Ryan raises his eyebrows almost on instinct) 

(Beat.)

DELILAH: Honestly, this is the most I've spoken to any guy. Like ever.

(Ryan involuntarily zones out. The thought arriving before he can stop it.)

(In his head. Her voice. "first dates end with sleeping".)

DELILAH: I almost wanted to call and cancel but you were so excited, and—

(Ryan still zoned out, her voice drifting into the background.)

DELILAH: ...but you seem like you have a very specific idea of what you're looking for. I'm sure you'll find her. I really do mean that. Again. I am sorry for wasting your time.

(She stands. Picks up her bag.)

DELILAH: I'm just going to freshen up and I'll head off. (planting a kiss on his cheek.) 

(She walks toward the back. Ryan sits alone. Picks up his phone. Calls Dan. Voicemail.)

RYAN: (quietly, into the phone) Hey. It's me. Don't... don't wait up.

(He sets the phone down. Stands. Straightens his jacket. Takes one step toward the exit. Stops. Looks back toward the restroom. Stands there for a moment.)

END TAG

(An empty table. Two unfinished desserts. A wine glass with her lipstick on it.)

(Locked Restroom somewhere in the back. A faint, unmistakable voice echoes.)

DELILAH: Oh my god. Yes. Yes. Yes. Ahhh!! Just like that. Oh, Ryan.

(Blackout.)
