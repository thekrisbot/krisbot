GROUNDED

Caught between a rock and a hard place.
July 13, 2026

CHARACTERS:

JEREMY. Mid-30s. A warm, bright, over-enthusiastic working actor. Commits completely to every note he's ever been given.

JENNY. 30s. Jeremy's long-time girlfriend.

BARRY. 30s. Jeremy’s best friend from school.

SUPPORTING CHARACTERS:

MARTIN. 50s. Casting director.

FATHER ALAN. 60s. A priest. 

SETTING: A casting office, then everyday places. Present day.

(Lights up. A folding table. MARTIN sits behind it with a pen. JEREMY stands, mid-audition.)

JEREMY

(intensely)

"The sediment doesn't lie, Frank. I am telling you, we have struck gold."

(A beat. Martin's impressed with the audition.)

MARTIN

That's... yeah. That's good. Very intense.

JEREMY

Thank you. I really wanted to get into character. You know? How would the geologist feel as he makes a groundbreaking discovery?

MARTIN

(flipping a page, not really looking up)

Sure... sure. I'll be honest, we definitely like what we saw today, but we are doing a couple of rounds of auditions. That said, I can confidently say that we want to see you here again for the callbacks. That's in six weeks. See if you can improve on this performance. Maybe add a character wrinkle. Really dig into the head of this geologist. Yeah?

JEREMY

(very pumped, fist clenched)

Yes, yes. Live as the character. Yep, I can do it.

MARTIN

Alright buddy, see you soon.

WEEK ONE. MONDAY. THE APARTMENT.

(Evening. JENNY on the couch with the phone. JEREMY enters from a walk, flushed with purpose, holding a smooth grey rock.)

JENNY

(not looking up)

Hey babe! Good walk?

JEREMY

Great walk! Look what I found by the canal.

JENNY

(gets up, plants a quick kiss)

Ooh what's that? A belated birthday present?

JEREMY

(holding up the rock)

Aww. Not exactly. You know how I have the callback for the biopic I auditioned for? I thought it would be a fun little prop. You know. Hold a rock. Think like a geologist.

JENNY

Aww, that is so cute babe. Does the little guy have a name?

JEREMY

Huh. Isn't that a little weird? Naming a rock?

(Jenny shrugs with a smile.)

JEREMY (CONT'D)

Fine, how about Buggy?

JENNY

I love that! Now Buggy, if you will excuse us...

(Takes Buggy off his hands and places it on the windowsill.)

JEREMY

Ooh, someone's in the mood. Dr. Jeremy reporting for booty.

JENNY

(excited, lovey-dovey)

Oooh, Dr. Jeremy. Talk about a rock and a hard place… oooh come here.

(Jenny gestures towards her thighs. The couple share a kiss as she leads him to the bedroom enthusiastically.)

WEEK ONE. THURSDAY. THE APARTMENT.

(Morning. Jenny making coffee. Jeremy at the door, keys in hand, work bag on. He pauses, realises he has forgotten something. Goes back. Taps Buggy on the windowsill twice, gently, and leaves. Jenny watches and smiles.It's cute.)

JEREMY

(to Buggy)

Bye, buddy. We'll practise the lines when I am back.

JENNY

(calling after him, amused)

Say bye to me too maybe?

JEREMY

(from offstage)

Bye babe! Love you!

(The door closes. Jenny looks at Buggy. Buggy looks at nothing in particular, because, well, he's a rock.)

WEEK TWO. TUESDAY. DATE NIGHT.

(BARRY drops off two menus at Jeremy and Jenny's usual table.)

BARRY

The usual table for the usual suspects. Let me guess, a cream of cheese soup for the beautiful Jenny, and a couple of water-soaked breadsticks for her stalker.

JENNY

Awww, thanks Bar.

(She plants a kiss on the cheek, as Barry gives a big hug to Jeremy. Jeremy takes Buggy from his jacket pocket and sets him beside the salt.)

BARRY

(a beat, pointing with his pen)

Is that a rock?

JEREMY

Who, Buggy? Oh yeah.

BARRY

(rolling his eyes)

He has a name? Jenny, I told you the acting career was going to get to him. It’s too late for me to get out.You should take the parachute while you still got the chance.

(Jenny chuckles. She's come to expect the banter between those two guys at this point.)

JENNY

No, Buggy is just this prop for a movie he auditioned for. Speaking of which, must he really be a plus one on our date night? It's bad enough we have Barry already.

BARRY

(deciding to accept this, as friends do)

Alright, that's my cue to take off. Say bye before you leave and I'll get Eduardo for the table.

(Barry goes. Jenny watches Jeremy set up a plate and cutlery for Buggy. She raises an eyebrow with a sheepish smile.)

JEREMY

(reading the menu)

What? This is how Brody won an Oscar for The Pianist, babe.

JENNY

(hands up in the air, still smiling)

I didn't say anything.

(She picks up her menu. Over the top of it, one more glance at the rock as they order.)

WEEK THREE. SUNDAY. A CHURCH.

(Jeremy is flanked by Jenny and what looks like an empty seat on first look. As a new person gets down to sit, the whole row adjusts, scooching over one seat.)

CHURCHGOER

Can I take this seat?

JEREMY

Oh sorry, it's taken.

CHURCHGOER

(looks around)

Oh, is someone coming?

JEREMY

(scoffs lightly, pointing at the seat with mild annoyance)

Yeah.

(The Churchgoer then notices a small rock on the seat. He's visibly confused but mutters to himself and lets it go.)

JENNY

(whispering)

Everything okay babe?

JEREMY

Yeah, yeah. Some crazy guy.

FATHER ALAN

(from the pulpit)

...and so we ask ourselves not "why me," but "what now." Let us pray.

(Heads bow. Jeremy gently tilts Buggy forward too, so the rock is also bowing. Jenny sees this out of the corner of her eye mid-prayer. The service continues.)

(FATHER ALAN comes over to greet the couple.)

FATHER ALAN

(shaking Jeremy's hand, glancing at the rock in his other hand)

And who is this?

JEREMY

Buggy, Father. He's part of a role I'm preparing for.

FATHER ALAN

(entirely unbothered)

Ah, the theatre. Well. The Lord's house has room for everyone. Good luck, son.

(Jeremy beams, vindicated by God himself. Jenny smiles out of politeness.)

WEEK THREE. THAT NIGHT. THE APARTMENT.

(Bedtime. Jenny in bed with a book. Through the doorway we can see Jeremy in the living room, carefully setting Buggy on the windowsill. He adjusts him. Adjusts him again. Whispers something we can't hear. Comes to bed.)

JENNY

(not looking up from her book)

What do you say to him?

JEREMY

(getting into bed)

What?

JENNY

At the window. Every night. You say something.

JEREMY

(blushing)

Oh, we've gotten into the habit of telling each other about our days.

JENNY

(a beat)

"Our"?

JEREMY

(matter of fact)

Huh-uh.

(Jenny looks at him. He sincerely kisses her cheek, rolls over, and is asleep in thirty seconds. Jenny stays sitting up. Her book is open. She is not reading it.)

WEEK FOUR. WEDNESDAY. THE APARTMENT.

(Evening. Jenny comes home with shopping bags. She hears the bath running. She goes to greet Jeremy but he's nowhere to be found.)

JENNY

(looking around)

Babe? The water's running! Are you there?

JEREMY

(from the kitchen, cheerful)

Hey babe, in here.

JENNY

(coming in for a hug)

Did you draw a bath for me?

JEREMY

Oh, actually it was for Buggy. But sure, you can take it I guess. I'll prepare him a new one. I accidentally poured too much of that lavender salt, anyway. That makes him dizzy. That reminds me, could you add the lemon flavour salts to the shopping list. That really soothes him.

JENNY

(shocked but still trying to keep her cool, grinding her teeth)

What?

JEREMY

(doesn't clock it)

Yeah.

JENNY

(takes a deep sigh)

Babe, you know how much I support your passion. But this thing(gesturing in the air)has gone way out of hand at this point.

JEREMY

(mildly surprised)

Wait, where is this coming from? Do you not like Buggy?

JENNY

(finally losing her cool)

This! This is what I am talking about! I don't "like" Buggy. I don't "hate" Buggy. I don't care about him! And he doesn't care either. Just like how he doesn't have a favourite Jonas brother. While we are at it, he doesn't think Bojack Horseman is overrated. Or..or.. (stammering) that it is inauthentic for James Bond to be played by a woman. You know why? You know why, Jeremy? You know why? BECAUSE HE IS A FRICKING ROCK. THAT'S IT. THAT'S ALL HE IS. AND I AM TIRED OF PRETENDING THAT HE IS SOMETHING MORE.

(Jeremy is speechless. Jenny has tears rolling down her eyes.)

JENNY

(realising what just happened, going in for a hug)

Babe, I am so sorry. I shouldn't have.

JEREMY

No, no. Can you just give us a minute?

(Jenny rolls her eyes, unsure what that means, and looks at Buggy in the bath as if he could get up and leave.)

JEREMY (CONT'D)

Oh sorry, I meant can you give us a minute?

(gesturing at himself and Buggy)

JENNY

(hands up in the air)

Unbelievable!

(Jenny shuts the bathroom door behind her, leaving Jeremy and Buggy alone as she hears Jeremy faintly through the door.)

WEEK FIVE. TUESDAY. THE RESTAURANT.

(The usual table. But tonight Jenny sits across from Jeremy AND Buggy as Barry makes his way over.)

BARRY

Hey, it's my favourite... woah. Everything okay?

JEREMY

Yeah, yeah, just a misunderstanding. Can you get us two waters please?

(Jenny doesn't say a word, sitting with her arms crossed. Barry clocks the situation and walks away.)

JEREMY

(to Jenny)

Oh, did you want anything?

(Jenny scoffs in disbelief.)

JENNY

Seriously!? You are icing me out?  

JEREMY

Alright, you wanna talk. I’ll talk. You were WAY OUT LINE today. 

(Jenny throws her hand up in air in disbelief, scoffing)

JENNY

I am out of line? You are the one who is letting a stupid dirty little rock come between us.  

(Jeremy is shocked and rushes to cover Buggy’s ears.)

JENNY CONT’D

Speaking of which, how many weeks has it been since you explored my bedrock now??

JEREMY
   (shocked)

You know that geologist died a celibate!

JENNY
  (sarcastically)

Shocking!You know what? I don't need to sit and be a third wheel in(gesturing at the duo)whatever this is. Why don't the two of you finish the rest of the date and I'll order takeout.

JEREMY

(arms crossed)

Fine by us!

(Jenny leaves the restaurant as Jeremy has a talk with Buggy, reassuring him that Jenny will come around.)

WEEK SIX. MONDAY. THE APARTMENT.

(Evening of the callback. Jeremy paces in his apartment, phone in hand. Buggy next to him.)

BARRY

(on phone)

And Jenny?

JEREMY

Nope, she's still at Sarah's.

BARRY

(on phone)

I cannot believe you are sabotaging your relationship for a stupid rock.

JEREMY

Yeah, well it is what it is. And if she cannot understand what this opportunity means, to hell with her. Anyway, got to go, I am expecting a call from casting anytime.

(He grins. Grabs Buggy.)

JEREMY

Buddy, this is it. Six weeks of losing myself in boulders, tectonic plates, and dust storms!!

(Phone rings. It's the casting people.)

JEREMY

Hey Martin! Yes, I've been waiting for your call all day! So when do you want me to come in for the callbacks?

(Martin says something inaudible on the other end of the phone.)

JEREMY

What? No, no. No way! This cannot be happening.

MARTIN

(on phone)

What can I say? Our research showed that there is simply no audience for a biopic on a geologist from the 1940s.

JEREMY

But I've spent the last six weeks preparing for this!! You cannot take it away from me.

MARTIN

(on phone)

Well, sorry mate. It is what it is. But hey, I really liked your energy and we'll keep you in mind if we have something in the future. We have this idea for a drama based on this lifeguard in Florida.. who spotted a fourteen-foot alligator in an indoor pool! Oooh, chills. Anyway, have a nice evening, Jeremy.

(Jeremy almost collapses to the floor hearing the news.)

LATER THAT EVENING. AT THE BAR.

(JEREMY sits alone at the bar, Buggy placed on a folded napkin beside his glass with the same ceremony as always. BARRY next to him, with a drink in hand.)

BARRY

So let's recap this.

(Jeremy nods.)

BARRY (CONT'D)

You auditioned for a movie based on a geologist nobody's heard of. Decided to "method act", carrying around a dumb rock everywhere, like it's your pet. You drove away a perfectly nice girl like Jenny, because of that dumb rock. To top it all off, the movie got cancelled. Because nobody wants to go see a movie about a geologist! That right?

(Jeremy nods again, staring into the drink.)

BARRY (CONT'D)

Do you hear how crazy you've been acting? I don't know what's going to happen with Jenny, Jer. But the first thing you gotta do is get rid of that damned rock.

(Jeremy nods one more time. He knows Barry is right. The two prepare to leave, and Jeremy, with a heavy heart, taps Buggy twice on the head and painfully gets up toward the exit.)

JEREMY
 Can you give us a minute?

(Barry scoffs but understands and leaves)

JEREMY (CONT’D)
 (breaking down almost)

Hey buddy!Thank you for the last six weeks. Oh this is harder than I thought.

(Jeremy drags himself out, leaving Buggy behind)

(As he leaves, DAHL, a middle-aged man who has been overhearing the conversation from a nearby stool, moves over to Jeremy's now-empty seat, where Buggy sits alone on the napkin.) 

DAHL

(to Buggy)

Hey buddy!

(Dahl makes a phone call to his wife, Marguerite.)

DAHL

(on the phone)

I just had the greatest idea in the world. PET ROCK!

(wildly gesturing in the air)

What do you mean, people don't want a rock for a pet? It's a home run!

(Dahl hangs up the phone. He sets the rock back down gently, almost reverently, and pulls a cocktail napkin toward him. Starts writing.)

DAHL

(writing, excitedly taps Buggy twice)

Buddy, you are going to make me a millionaire!!

(Blackout.)
