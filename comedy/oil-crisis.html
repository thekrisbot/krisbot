Oil crisis

Geopolitics 101
March 13, 2026

CHARACTERS

BRETT, DOM, CHARLIE — mid 30s. Friends of the groom. 

SOPHIE — early 30s. Friend of the bride. 

MATT — the Groom.

TARA — the Bride. 

SETTING: Table five at the wedding reception. Dessert cleared. Sophie, wine in hand, finds herself in the middle of an intense conversation between the guys about the oil crisis.

(Lights up. Brett is going. Dom is nodding. Charlie is leaning back having just made a point) 

BRETT: — and look, the strike was inevitable. The moment Trump took out Khamenei, Iran had to respond. You poke the bear, the bear closes the Strait. That's just geopolitics 101.

DOM: And now oil's at a hundred. No one saw it coming!

SOPHIE: (pleasantly) I think the Strait has actually been a flagged risk for a long time and every major energy body has had scenarios for this—

BRETT: Right, exactly, which is why it's so insane that nobody hedged against it.

SOPHIE: (beat) Oh. I was going to say the opposite actually but anyway.. I was talking to a couple of colleagues. Their view is that the most underpriced risk right now isn't even oil. It’s fertilisers. Because about a third of that market moves through the Strait and If this disruption carries into spring planting—

BRETT: ( dismissively grunting) “Fertilisers”. Nonsense. Sophie, let me give you a piece of advise you will thank me for — stay away from people with these “hot takes”. They hype up all these random stocks and products. You listen to them and purchase an option or something and three weeks later, you are holding the bag. That’s why I have one rule. If you don’t have a 100K followers (pausing) at a minimum, you don’t have my attention. 

DOM: Sophie, you gotta get on X. That’s where all the 4-D chess happens. You should follow this guy; MarketHawk. Just this morning he posted a thread dismantling the price cap argument. Totally changes your world-view (mimicking exploding head)

SOPHIE: (interested) Oooh what does he do?

DOM: He is a finfluencer! The guy’s a genius. Started posting memes on Doge during college and two years later he’s got 224K followers and a blue tick. Legit.

SOPHIE: (beat) I mean..eight dollars gets you a blue—

CHARLIE: (ignoring Sophie) Bruh. That was last week. He’s up to 228 now.  

SOPHIE: (patiently trying) What's his take though?

DOM: It's a lot of technical details. You have to be in the weeds but basically the price cap is just vibes. India's buying, China's buying, nobody cares about the cap. 

SOPHIE: (carefully) I mean.. isn't that a little (searches for the right word, too polite to land on the one she wants) ...reductive? 

BRETT: Woah! This is a strict no-MarketHawk-slander zone. (gesturing the space around) 

DOM: There is also this other guy I just found a couple of days ago. Jamie something... (snapping fingers trying to remember) Chang! Jamie Chang! He had this piece on hardware revival that’s just.. (gesturing great) 

SOPHIE: (slowly) Jamie Chen? 

DOM: Yeah, could be.

SOPHIE: Oh Jamie’s actually— 

DOM: Dude has like only 10K subscribers but I think he is gonna blow up soon… like the next Michael Burry in finance. 

SOPHIE: (carefully) She.

DOM: Huh?

SOPHIE: Jamie Chen? She’s a woman. She was with the FT and went independent recently… we actually discuss her writing in our—

DOM: Oh, that’s cool (moving on) so you from around here? 

SOPHIE: (slightly surprised to be asked) Oh, no. I am based out of Boston… I had a work thing this week and the timing worked out with the wedding thankfully.

(Charlie slams the table before making a point) 

CHARLIE: You know what I hate? It’s like, this entire liquidity crisis could have been avoided by simply preparing ourselves... 

SOPHIE: (confused but polite) Liquidity? Isn’t that more of a credit market term?

DOM: It's a crisis in the liquid energy sense.

CHARLIE: Precisely. This is COVID all over again. You are caught off guard because you failed to prepare and now the whole thing’s blown up in your face!

SOPHIE: (cautiously) Well to be fair, COVID-19 was a black swan. Meanwhile, the Strait has been a documented risk for thirty years. It's the most war-gamed chokepoint in energy security. In a way, the surprise would have been if it didn't—

BRETT (engrossed in phone, looking up) Is that on Hulu? 

SOPHIE: (pause) I am sorry? 

BRETT:  You guys were talking about some tv show… Black Swan? That on Hulu?

SOPHIE: (exasperated) Oh! Black Swan? That’s a book. By Nassim Taleb? Talks about— 

BRETT: Right, call me when they turn that into a movie.

DOM: (to Sophie, warmly) It's really great that you're showing interest though. 

SOPHIE: Ummm thank you? 

(Brett interrupts)

BRETT: Seriously. Most women just tune out when you talk about anything brainy. It’s like guys or Taylor Swift or guys (mocking hand gesture)

(Sophie fuming, but decides to disengage)

SOPHIE: Anyway, I have to get going. It was lovely (enunciating sarcastically) meeting you guys. (Walking away already) 

BRETT: Totally! We should catch-up sometime.. I’ll get your number from Matt! 

(Beat.)

BRETT: (flagging the Groom as he passes) Matt!! That chick, Sophie. 

(Pointing to her from a distance) How do we know her?

GROOM: Oh, I don’t know man. Some friend of Tara’s (gone)

(Tara sweeps past in full bride mode)

BRETT: Tara! This Sophie (lowering his voice, gesturing in that direction) 

Is she... (raises his hand, like he's measuring height but for intellect)

TARA: (stopping, lighting up) Oh my god Sophie?! She's amazing. We were in the same dorm at Oxford. Did you meet her? Oooh ask her where she works, we always tease her about it.

BRETT: Why? 

TARA: Well, whenever someone asks about work she just says she teaches. But she is actually a Ha— (looking at the next group waving impatiently at her). Okay got to go, just talk to her!

(Silence.)

CHARLIE: (slowly) Did you catch that?

DOM: (slowly) Not fully. I think she said she is a school teacher?

CHARLIE: Huh. No wonder she looked like was struggling to keep up. 

(Blackout.)
